1. Fix the Canvas (Create Depth)
Professional dashboards use subtle background colors to make the data cards "pop" forward.

The Background: Change the main application background (the area behind the cards and the header) from pure white to a very soft, cool off-white/cream (Hex: #F4F7F9 or #F8F9FA).

The Cards: Keep the data cards pure white (#FFFFFF).

The Shadows: Remove the harsh gray borders around the cards. Instead, use a very soft, blurry drop shadow. (CSS example: box-shadow: 0px 4px 20px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.03); border-radius: 12px;). This creates the illusion that the white cards are floating above the dashboard.

2. Anchor the Sidebar (Fix Contrast)
The sidebar needs to feel like the structural foundation of the app, not empty space.

Text & Icons: Change the inactive sidebar text and icons to a solid, legible Charcoal Gray (#5A6474).

Active State: For the page you are currently on ("Dashboard Overview"), give the entire row a soft Light Blue background (#E6F0F9), make the text darker/bolder (#1E293B), and change the icon to your active Sage Green or a Deep Blue.

Separation: Ensure there is a crisp 1px border (#E2E8F0) separating the sidebar and the top header from the main gray canvas.

3. Redesign the KPI Cards (The "Bento" Layout)
Right now, the alignment inside your "Financial Vital Signs" cards is scattered. An analytical card needs strict alignment. Rebuild the inside of each card like this:

Top Row (Context): Place the title (e.g., "Total Fee Collection") in the top left in a medium gray (#64748B), size 13px. Place the icon in the top right. Pro tip: Put the icon inside a small, softly colored circle background (e.g., a light green circle for the green wallet icon) to make it look polished.

Middle Row (The Data): Left-align the main number (₹12.4Cr) directly under the title. Make it large (28px-32px), bold, and dark Charcoal (#1E293B).

Bottom Row (The Trend): Keep the trend indicator (↑ 2% Above Benchmark) directly under the main number.

The Analytical Upgrade: To make it truly professional, add a Sparkline (a tiny, simple line graph with no axes) at the very bottom of the card showing the 30-day trend. Use a soft green line for positive trends and orange for negative.

4. Clean Up the Top Header
Search Bar: Make the background of the search bar a slightly darker gray than the header (#F1F5F9) so it looks like an inset input field.

Export Button: It currently looks disabled. Give it a subtle gray outline (border: 1px solid #CBD5E1) and dark text.

Date Selector: Make sure the text alignment inside the dropdown box is vertically centered.
